TFTs via Cyclic and Modular Infinity Operads

Introduction

Abstract

Modular (-)operads provide a (coherent) algebraic way to encode the gluing operations of connected surfaces. In particular, the bordism ()-category provides a special kind of modular operad whose algebras are topological field theories, where taking algebras in the symmetric monoidal -category of -linear categories recovers Segal's notion of modular functors, used in his approach to conformal field theories. If instead the algebras are taken in the derived category of chain complexes over , then one obtains a structure closely related to Witten's notion of cohomological field theories. In this talk we aim to provide an example driven introduction to modular (-)operads and their appearance in the study of topological field theories.

What are (-)Operads?

When working with higher algebraic structure, especially algebraic structure which is homotopical in nature, such as that associated with cohomological field theories, it is beneficial to have concrete combinatorial descriptions of your data for the sake of computations. Note that (-)categories are modelled on the idea that we have elementary structures, given by objects and arrows , and that we have compositional structure allowing us to build an arrow from a sequence of arrows , where when this is the same as an object . One can think of a category as a collection of sets indexed by these structures, which corresponds to a functor , satisfying the additional gluing requirement
